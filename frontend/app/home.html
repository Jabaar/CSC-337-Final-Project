Israel-Hamas Truce Reaches Fifth Day After Extension Deal

November 30, 2023
Topics: World and Politics

Source: NBC News

Background

The truce between Israel and Hamas in the Gaza Strip has extended into a fifth day, with more exchanges of prisoners and hostages expected. Israel has pledged to continue its pause in attacks on Hamas as long as hostages are being released. The United States has welcomed the extended cessation in fighting and continues diplomatic efforts for the release of all hostages.

Summary

Israel and Hamas have agreed to extend the truce for two more days, with more prisoners and hostages being exchanged. The United States supports this extension and calls for the release of all hostages held by Hamas. Despite the pause in fighting, the ultimate goal of dismantling Hamas and its rule over Gaza remains. The international community, including the United Nations, is working to alleviate the humanitarian crisis in Gaza. China will preside over a UN Security Council meeting to address the Israel-Hamas conflict and seek a prolonged ceasefire
